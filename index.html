01 / the avatar

A body that keeps the score.

Log a workout and watch the exact muscles you trained heat up on your avatar. Overdo it and they burn red. Skip a week and watch the map cool. No charts to decode — it's you.

02 / logging

Log anything in seconds.

Photo-first meals with automatic EXIF stripping. Any workout — barbell to badminton — with a picker that knows which muscles each exercise hits. N identical sets in one tap.

03 / leaderboards

Compete without being watched.

World, country, and city leaderboards built on privacy: you choose what's visible, scoring runs on anonymous MET-minutes, and your data stays yours. Friends join by QR code.

04 / squad alarms

Your squad won't let you sleep in.

Squad Alarms are peer-to-peer wake-up pacts — no server, just your crew. Full-screen alarm, and the only snooze button is doing the movement the sensors are checking for.

05 / the payoff

Joy you actually earned.

Streaks with freezes for life's bad weeks. XP and levels tuned to habit science. And when you hit a real PR — confetti. Real celebrations for real work.

WatermelonDB → Supabase RLS

The whole product is offline-first by construction. A local SQLite database (WatermelonDB) is the source of truth; a hand-written synchronize() layer reconciles with Supabase Edge Functions over a privacy-preserving, row-level-security model, and a nightly pg_cron job aggregates anonymous score events into the world / country / city leaderboards. Photos go to zero-egress object storage.

Designed, built, and adversarially reviewed with an agentic SDLC — every phase gated by tests, lint, and independent AI review. Source available to judges on request.
